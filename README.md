**# Doctoral Meta-Analyses 2: Quantum Biology & Bioengineering**

A curated collection of comprehensive, doctoral-level meta-analyses exploring the intersection of quantum effects in biological systems and advanced bioengineering applications.

## Overview

This repository compiles in-depth, evidence-based meta-analyses on frontier topics where quantum mechanics meets biology and where cutting-edge gene-editing technologies demand rigorous risk assessment. Each paper synthesizes hundreds of studies (spanning experimental, computational, and regulatory literature up to mid-2026), providing balanced overviews suitable for researchers, graduate students, and interdisciplinary scholars.

**Theme**: From nature's evolved quantum strategies in living systems to the responsible engineering of genomes, these works examine how quantum and molecular phenomena shape efficiency, robustness, and safety in biology.

## Contents

### Quantum Biology Series
- **[Photosynthetic Quantum Coherence](./Photosynthetic_Quantum_Coherence.md)**  
  Meta-analysis on excitonic energy transfer in light-harvesting complexes (FMO, LHCII, LH2, etc.). Covers 2D spectroscopy evidence, vibronic vs. electronic coherence, environment-assisted quantum transport (ENAQT), and implications for artificial photosynthesis.

- **[Magnetoreception Dynamics](./Magnetoreception_Cryptochrome_Radical_Pairs.md)**  
  In-depth evaluation of cryptochrome proteins and the radical pair mechanism in migratory birds and other species. Includes photochemistry, spin dynamics, protein interactions, behavioral evidence, and open questions in quantum sensing.

- **[Enzymatic Quantum Tunneling](./Enzymatic_Quantum_Tunneling.md)**  
  Synthesis of kinetic data on hydrogen (proton/hydride/H-atom) and electron tunneling in enzymes. Features anomalous kinetic isotope effects (KIEs), protein dynamics, Marcus theory applications, rate enhancements, and evolutionary optimization.

### Bioengineering & Compliance Series
- **[CRISPR Bioengineering Compliance](./CRISPR_NHEJ_Risks_Compliance.md)**  
  Global synthesis of gene-editing literature focusing on risk patterns in non-homologous end joining (NHEJ). Maps large deletions, structural variants, chromothripsis, p53 responses, off-target effects, mitigation strategies, and regulatory landscapes across jurisdictions.
